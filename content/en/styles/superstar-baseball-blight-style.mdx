## Mastering the Hitting Mechanics with Blight

Having the **superstar baseball blight style** equipped is only half the battle. To truly hit "insane" home runs, you must master the two pillars of hitting: timing and coordination. The Blight style increases your power, but it also requires you to be precise with your contact point.

### Timing Your Swing

Timing refers to when you click or press the swing button relative to the ball's arrival in the strike zone. 
*   **Early Swing:** Pulls the ball to the left (for right-handed hitters).
*   **Late Swing:** Pushes the ball to the right (for right-handed hitters).
*   **Perfect Timing:** Results in maximum exit velocity and a straight-away center field shot.

### Coordination and Placement

Coordination involves moving your hitting reticle to match the ball's location. The **superstar baseball blight style** rewards players who can "center" the ball. If you hit the ball with the edges of your reticle, you will likely result in a pop-fly or a ground-out, regardless of how much power your style provides.

| Contact Quality | Result | Chance of Home Run |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Perfect/Perfect** | Screaming Line Drive | 95% |
| **Good/Slightly Off** | Deep Fly Ball | 60% |
| **Late/Under** | Pop-up | 5% |
| **Early/Over** | Grounder | 0% |

> **⚠️ Warning:** Do not rely solely on your style. Even with the best power buffs, poor coordination will result in a "dookie" hitting performance.

**Q: Does the Blight style affect my fielding?**

**Q: Why am I still hitting pop-flies with a power style equipped?**
