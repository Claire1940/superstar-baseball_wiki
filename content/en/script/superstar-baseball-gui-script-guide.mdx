## Mastering the Batter's Box

Batting in Superstar Baseball is an art form that relies heavily on timing and pitch recognition. The game offers two primary methods of swinging, each suited for different situations and skill levels. Beginners should always start with the Contact Swing to build a foundation before moving on to the high-risk, high-reward Power Swing.

### Contact vs. Power Swings

The Contact Swing is the most reliable way to get on base. It features a larger hit window and is more forgiving if your timing is slightly off. Conversely, the Power Swing is designed for intermediate and professional players who can accurately predict the ball's trajectory. Successfully landing a Power Swing often results in a home run, but it requires much tighter timing.

| Swing Type | Difficulty | Accuracy | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Contact** | Low | High | Getting on base, 2-strike counts |
| **Power** | High | Low | Scoring runs, hitting home runs |

> **💡 Pro Tip:** Pay close attention to the sound of the hit. A "strong" hitting sound indicates a perfect swing, often leading to a home run. A "normal" sound suggests you were slightly early, resulting in shorter distances.

### Identifying the Strike Zone

The strike zone is represented by a square on your screen. Any pitch that lands inside this square is a strike if you don't swing. If a pitch is outside the square, it is a ball. Learning to "take" balls is just as important as swinging at strikes, as it forces the pitcher to throw more predictable pitches.

## Pitching Strategy and Ball Movement

Pitching is the defensive backbone of your team. A skilled pitcher can confuse even the best batters by varying speed and movement. In the current 2026 meta, relying on a single pitch type is a recipe for disaster. You must rotate through your arsenal to keep the opponent guessing.

### Pitch Types and Their Effects

Understanding the physics of each pitch is the first step toward becoming a pro pitcher. Each pitch has a specific movement pattern that can be exploited to force "swings and misses."

| Pitch Name | Speed | Movement | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fastball** | Very High | Straight | Medium |
| **Curveball** | Low | Significant Drop | High |
| **Changeup** | Very Low | Slow Straight | High |
| **Slider** | High | Lateral Break | Medium |
| **Sinker** | Medium | Sudden Drop | Medium |

> **Warning:** While slow pitches like the Changeup can confuse a batter's timing, they carry a higher risk of being hit for a home run if the batter stays patient and waits for the ball.

### Advanced Pitching Tactics

To truly excel, you should use the "Fast Pitch" to catch players off guard and the "Slow Pitch" to disrupt their rhythm. Pro batters in 2026 are exceptionally good at tracking the ball, so mixing your locations—hitting the corners of the strike zone—is essential for survival.
