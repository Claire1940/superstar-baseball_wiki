## Installing Project Rio on Windows and Mac

Project Rio is a specialized fork of the Dolphin emulator optimized for netplay. The installation process varies slightly depending on your operating system.

### Windows Installation Steps
1. Download the Project Rio executable from the official website.
2. If Windows Defender flags the file, click "More Info" and "Run Anyway."
3. Choose your installation path and complete the wizard.
4. Launch the application and double-click the center screen to set your ISO directory.

### macOS Installation Steps
1. Download the Project Rio ZIP file and extract it via Finder (avoid extracting directly in the browser downloads menu to prevent glitches).
2. Drag the application to your "Applications" folder.
3. If you receive a developer verification error, navigate to **System Settings > Privacy & Security** and select "Open Anyway" at the bottom of the menu.
4. If prompted, install Rosetta 2 to ensure compatibility with Intel-based code.

## Controller Configuration and Drivers

For the most authentic experience, many players prefer using an original GameCube controller. However, this requires a USB adapter (such as Mayflash or the official Wii U adapter) and specific drivers to eliminate input lag.

### Setting Up the GameCube Adapter

| Platform | Required Software | Key Setting |
|---|---|---|
| **Windows** | Zadig | Change driver to **WinUSB** |
| **Mac** | GC Adapter Driver | Toggle "On" in System Settings |
| **Both** | Physical Switch | Set adapter to **Wii U Mode** |

If you are using a modern controller like a PlayStation 5 or Xbox Series X gamepad, you do not need special drivers. Simply go to the "Controllers" menu in Project Rio, select "Standard Controller," and click "Configure." You will need to manually map your buttons by clicking the input field and pressing the corresponding button on your controller.

> **💡 Tip:** When mapping an Xbox or PlayStation controller, ensure you calibrate the "Deadzone" for your analog sticks to prevent "stick drift" during high-pressure pitching moments.

## Graphic Optimization for Online Play

To ensure a smooth experience for both you and your opponent, you must optimize your graphics settings. Desyncs are often caused by one player's computer failing to maintain 100% game speed.

### Recommended Graphics Backends
*   **macOS:** Always use **Metal**. While older guides suggested Vulcan, Metal is now natively supported and provides the best stability on Apple hardware.
*   **Windows:** **Direct3D 11** or **Direct3D 12** are typically the most stable. If you have an older GPU, Vulcan may offer a slight performance boost.

### Performance Tweak Table

| Setting | Recommendation | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Internal Resolution** | 1x or 2x Native | Higher values look better but tax the GPU. |
| **Full Screen** | Use "Full Screen" Button | Improves FPS stability over windowed mode. |
| **Show FPS** | Enabled | Helps you monitor if your game is dipping below 60. |
| **Audio Stretching** | Disabled | Can cause audio lag in netplay environments. |

## FAQ

**Q: Why is my game lagging even though I have a fast PC?**

A: In online play, lag is often caused by the connection between players rather than hardware. This **superstar baseball wiki** highly recommends using a wired Ethernet connection. Wi-Fi is inherently unstable and causes "stuttering" that can ruin the timing of a swing. If your "Ping" is consistently over 90ms, you may experience noticeable delay.

**Q: Can I play Mario Superstar Baseball Online on my Steam Deck?**

A: Yes! Since Project Rio is based on Dolphin, it can run on Linux-based systems like the Steam Deck. You will need to use Desktop Mode to install the Flatpak version or follow specific community scripts to get the Project Rio fork running.

**Q: What is the difference between "Stars On" and "Stars Off"?**

A: "Stars Off" removes the Star Skills (special moves) from the game, focusing entirely on pure mechanical skill and drafting. "Stars On" (often played in the Big Balla format) allows players to use Star Points for powerful hits and pitches, adding a layer of resource management to the strategy.

**Q: My GameCube controller isn't being detected on my Mac. What should I do?**

A: Ensure you have granted the "GC Adapter Driver" permission in your System Settings under "Login Items & Extensions." You must also restart your computer after the initial installation for the driver to initialize properly. Always check that your physical adapter is switched to "Wii U" mode and not "PC" mode.
